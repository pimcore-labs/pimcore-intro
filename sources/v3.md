# Pimcore Platform Cockpit — Concept & Specification (v3)

A 16:9 single-page web application styled as a high-end audio plugin (VST/AU instrument). Pimcore's six platform capabilities sit as colored modules on the left, two large knobs in the center drive **PXM** and **Agents**, an industry preset selector with hardware-style LEDs lives at the top, and the Pimcore **Data Spine** is visualized as a live signal-flow strip across the bottom. The cockpit lets a viewer dial in any combination of capabilities, agents intensity, and target industry, and instantly see the resulting use cases, business benefits, ICP, buyer personas, and matching real customer case studies.

The aesthetic shifts from the dark mil-spec cockpit of v1/v2 to a **light, premium, skeuomorphic instrument panel** — soft off-white background, brushed-aluminum knobs, pastel module cards, real shadows. The reference is contemporary high-end audio plugin design (Spectra One, FabFilter, UAD, Arturia) — premium, tactile, craft-forward.

**v3 changes from v2:**
- Visual system completely redesigned around skeuomorphic VST aesthetic.
- Two hero knobs — PXM (center, master control) and Agents (continuous intensity).
- Agentic PXM elevated to a first-class control surface, no longer a future-state hook.
- Industry selection becomes a hardware preset selector with LED indicators (no longer a side-rail list).
- Data Spine visualized as bottom-strip signal flow (replaces the simple progress meter).
- Capability connection lines from PXM knob to each module card make the "one platform" claim visible.

---

## 1. Purpose & Audiences

The cockpit exists to solve a single rhetorical problem: Pimcore's "any data, any industry, any channel" platform story is true and powerful, but flat slides cannot show the *combinatorial* nature of the platform — the fact that PIM × DAM × DXP for a manufacturer is a different value story than PIM × Commerce for a wholesaler, and that all six capabilities used together produce PXM, which is more than the sum of its parts.

The cockpit makes this story tactile. The viewer drives. They see the platform reconfigure itself to their world.

**Primary audiences**

- **Investors (PSG and similar Tier-1 PE/strategic).** Used live in management-presentation Q&A or as pre-read material. Demonstrates platform depth and forward thesis (Agents) without forcing a long product tour.
- **C-level prospects.** Used by AEs in discovery sessions to map Pimcore's capability surface against the prospect's specific industry context.
- **Partner enablement.** Used by SI partners as a conversation tool with their own clients.
- **Internal alignment.** Used inside Pimcore as a shared mental model for how capabilities map to industries.

**Non-audiences** (deliberate)

- End users / practitioners. This is not a demo of the product; it is a demo of the *positioning*.
- Highly technical buyers wanting a feature deep-dive. Pimcore Studio and live demos serve that purpose.

## 2. Concept Vision

### 2.1 The metaphor

A high-end audio plugin window — the kind of professional instrument software that producers and engineers use, where the visual quality of the UI signals the quality of the underlying engineering. Each capability is a "module" on the rack, with its own color identity and individual controls. Two large hero knobs — **PXM** and **AGENTS** — drive the platform's master state. The **Data Spine** runs across the bottom as a live signal-flow visualization, showing data entering, flowing through the spine, and leaving via channels. Industry presets live at the top, selected via a hardware-style LED dial.

### 2.2 Why the VST aesthetic

The dark cockpit aesthetic is everywhere in B2B SaaS demos. The light skeuomorphic instrument is rare in enterprise software and signals something different: that the people who built this care about craft. It softens the pitch from "high-tech enterprise" to "confident, mature, well-made." For investor and senior-buyer audiences the second reading is more powerful. The aesthetic itself does work for the brand.

### 2.3 The two moments that have to land

Two interconnected moments, not one:

1. **PXM Mode Engaged** — when all six capability modules are active, the PXM hero knob's halo ring transitions from purple to a full-spectrum gradient incorporating all six module colors, the connection lines from the central knob to each module light up at full intensity, and a subtle "PXM MODE" status indicator illuminates. Quietly elegant, no neon flash.

2. **Agentic PXM** — when the Agents knob is turned past 50%, small orchestration pulses begin traveling along the Data Spine flow lines. Above 80%, the spine itself takes on a faint amber/gold halo and the "AGENTIC" indicator on the spine activates. This is the visual proof that agents aren't a feature bolted on — they orchestrate the entire platform.

When both happen simultaneously (six modules + agents at high intensity), the cockpit is in its full final state: **Agentic PXM**, every capability engaged, agents orchestrating across the spine, presented as the strategic endpoint of the platform thesis.

### 2.4 Tone

- Premium instrument, not gamified gadget.
- Restraint over flourish. Soft shadows, gentle animations, never bouncy or playful.
- Tactile and physical — knobs feel like they have weight, modules feel like they sit on the chassis.
- Pimcore brand-coherent but airy: deep purple as accent rather than dominant. Pastel module colors carry visual identity. Off-white chassis carries the calm.

## 4. The Six Capability Modules (Left Rail)

Each capability is a self-contained module card stacked vertically in the left rail. The design closely echoes the reference's "1 FILTER / 2 DELAY / 3 REVERB / 4 DISTORTION" cards but extended to six modules and rebranded for Pimcore's domains.

### 4.1 Module structure (each card)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  [#] [icon]  PIM            [power]     │  ← Module header
│              Product Information         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   ─⌒─        ─⌒─        ─⌒─            │  ← Three knobs
│  CONTROL    CONTROL    CONTROL          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Each module card carries:

- **Number badge** (1–6) in the module's accent color, large
- **Icon** — abstract domain glyph (small, the module's accent color)
- **Module name** (bold, dark text)
- **Sub-label** — full domain name (smaller, muted)
- **Power button** (top-right) — circular toggle, illuminates in module color when on
- **Three sub-control knobs** (skeuomorphic brushed-aluminum, small) — each labeled in mono micro-text

The module sits on a pastel-tinted card background that uses a *very soft* version of the module's accent color (think 5–8% saturation). When the module is powered on, the card brightens slightly and a thin border in the accent color appears.

### 4.2 The six modules

| # | Module | Accent color | Card background | Three sub-controls | Gartner MQ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **PIM** — Product Information | `#5B8DEF` cool blue | very soft blue tint | Models · Channels · Variants | ◐ Entry 2026 |
| 2 | **MDM** — Master Data | `#9E7BD9` lavender | very soft lavender tint | Domains · Quality · Lineage | ✓ Current |
| 3 | **DAM** — Digital Asset | `#F38B6C` coral | very soft peach tint | Assets · Formats · Rights | — |
| 4 | **CDP** — Customer Data | `#7BC9A7` sage green | very soft mint tint | Sources · Identity · Segments | — |
| 5 | **DXP** — Digital Experience | `#A06CD5` warm violet | very soft violet tint | Sites · Personalization · Content | ✓ Current |
| 6 | **COMMERCE** — Digital Commerce | `#E8B547` warm amber | very soft cream tint | Catalog · Checkout · Channels | — |

Pimcore Purple `#5924AB` is reserved for the **PXM** central knob and platform-wide signature elements — not used as a module color. This keeps the platform layer visually distinct from the capability layer.

The six accent colors are deliberately chosen to feel coherent (similar saturation and value ranges, all tuned to the off-white chassis) while giving each module an unambiguous identity.

### 4.3 Module states

- **Off (default).** Card very pale, accent muted, power button gray, sub-knobs dimmed. Connection line to PXM is invisible.
- **Hover.** Card lifts slightly (1–2px shadow increase), border faintly visible.
- **On.** Card brightens, accent border visible, power button illuminates in accent color, sub-knobs become live, connection line to PXM lights up in accent color.
- **On + agentized** (Agents > 50% AND module is on). A subtle amber pulse travels along the connection line to PXM, indicating the agent layer is actively orchestrating that module.

### 4.4 Sub-control knobs

Cosmetic only in v1 — the three sub-control knobs per module exist to make the module card feel like a real instrument and to add the visual texture of a control surface. They show small position indicators (a dot at "12 o'clock" by default) but do not need to drive functionality. The cockpit's actual computed state derives from module on/off plus the two hero knobs.

A v2 enhancement could give them light interactivity (e.g., adjusting "Channels" sub-knob on PIM cycles the right-panel content through different channel scenarios), but this is not v1 scope.

### 4.5 The PIM/MDM tagging note

The case study database tags PIM and MDM as a single category (`pim-mdm`). The hexagon/rail keeps them as **separate modules** because the marketing taxonomy is six. The case-study filter merges them transparently when filtering. See Section 9.2.

## 5. The Two Hero Knobs (Center Stage)

Two large brushed-aluminum knobs occupy the center stage, side by side. They are the cockpit's primary interactive controls and the visual focal points.

### 5.1 PXM knob (left center)

**The platform master control.** Larger of the two (≈ 320px diameter). Positioned slightly above center vertically.

**Visual structure:**
- Brushed-aluminum knob face, soft metallic highlight at 30°/30°.
- Surrounding **halo ring** that fills as capabilities engage. The halo is segmented into six arcs (one per capability), each arc fills in its module's accent color when the corresponding module is on.
- Tick marks around the knob (24 marks) for visual instrument-quality finish.
- Pointer notch on the knob itself indicates rotation position.
- Center label `PXM` in large display type below the knob.
- Sub-label `Pimcore Experience Management` in mono micro-text.
- Status callsign below: `STANDBY` / `PARTIAL — 3/6` / `EXPANSION — 5/6` / `PXM MODE ENGAGED`.

**Behavior — interpretation B (both passive and active):**

The knob has dual behavior. It is both a *receiver* (its halo reflects which modules are on, regardless of how they were turned on) and a *master control* (rotating the knob progressively engages capabilities in a defined order).

- **Receiver behavior.** When a module is powered on/off via its individual power button, the corresponding halo arc fills/empties in that module's color. The PXM knob's pointer rotates to the position matching the count of active capabilities.
- **Master control behavior.** Click-and-drag rotation on the knob itself engages capabilities progressively. The engagement order (default): PIM → MDM → DAM → CDP → DXP → Commerce, clockwise from minimum. Each "click" of rotation adds the next capability and powers on the corresponding module card.
- **Reconciliation.** State is stored as an *ordered set* of active modules — when you rotate the knob to position 4, the first 4 in the engagement order are on; if you then individually toggle off PIM via its module card, the knob's halo updates to show 3 active arcs and the pointer drops to position 3, but the *remaining* active set keeps MDM, DAM, CDP rather than reverting to a default order.

The engagement order can be made configurable per session (drag-to-reorder modules in the rail), but v1 ships with the default order above.

**The "all six" moment.** When all six capabilities are active, the halo ring transitions from individual colored arcs to a unified Pimcore Purple gradient (in the brand color), a subtle pulse animation begins, and the status callsign reads `PXM MODE ENGAGED` in deeper purple. The knob face itself takes on a faint purple tint.

### 5.2 Agents knob (right center)

**The agentic intensity control.** Slightly smaller than the PXM knob (≈ 240px diameter). Positioned right-of-center, vertically centered against the PXM knob.

**Visual structure:**
- Brushed-aluminum knob face, identical material treatment.
- Halo ring is a single continuous arc (not segmented), filling as the knob rotates.
- Halo color: amber/gold gradient (`#E8B547` → `#F5C842`) — the strategic forward-thesis color.
- Tick marks (16 marks) around the perimeter.
- Center label `AGENTS` in display type below the knob.
- Sub-label `Agentic PXM` in mono micro-text.
- Numeric readout: percent (0% – 100%) directly below.
- Status callsign: `OFF` / `ASSIST` (1–33%) / `CO-PILOT` (34–66%) / `AUTONOMOUS` (67–100%).

**Behavior:**
- Continuous intensity, 0–100%.
- Click-and-drag rotation, like the PXM knob.
- At any value above 0, the Data Spine strip begins showing orchestration pulses traveling along its flow lines (see Section 7.4). The intensity and frequency of these pulses scale with the Agents knob value.
- At ≥ 50%, a subtle amber pulse begins traveling along the connection line from the PXM knob to each active module, signaling that agents are orchestrating across the platform.
- At ≥ 80%, the spine itself takes on a faint amber halo and an `AGENTIC` indicator on the spine illuminates.

The Agents knob can be turned independently of which modules are active. Even with zero modules active, the knob is still functional — it just produces no visible orchestration effect because there's nothing to orchestrate.

### 5.3 Connection lines from PXM to modules

The center-stage region contains a thin, elegant **connection line** drawn from the PXM knob's center outward to each of the six capability module cards in the left rail. These lines are the visual proof that the modules are part of one platform.

**Specification:**
- Drawn as smooth curved bezier paths from the PXM knob's left edge to the right edge of each module card.
- Default state (module off): line is barely visible, ~10% opacity, neutral gray.
- Active state (module on): line illuminates in the module's accent color, ~60% opacity, with a soft glow.
- Agentized state (module on AND Agents > 50%): a small amber pulse travels along the line at a frequency proportional to the Agents knob value (slow at 50%, fast at 100%).
- All six active: the connection lines together form a fan from the PXM knob — the visual signature of "PXM Mode Engaged."

These lines are SVG paths rendered behind the module cards and the central knobs but above the chassis background. They never cross the right-panel area or the spine strip.

### 5.4 Capability headlines panel (below the two knobs)

Below the hero knobs, occupying the lower portion of the center stage, is a small panel that surfaces capability headlines for the currently active modules. Format echoes the right-panel sourcing pattern from v2 §6.1, but compressed:

```
ACTIVE CAPABILITIES — 3 DOMAINS                     [ EXPAND ↗ ]

[PIM]   Advanced Data Modeling   ·   Multi-Channel Publishing   ·   Data Distribution
[DAM]   Brand Portals   ·   Asset Conversions   ·   Streamlined Asset Access
[DXP]   Personalization   ·   Content Modelling   ·   Website Management
```

Three headlines per active module, separated by middle dots, all from the canonical capability export. Compact horizontal layout. The "EXPAND" link could open a modal with full descriptions (v2 enhancement).

When zero modules are active: `▶ ENGAGE A MODULE TO VIEW CAPABILITIES`.

Ten LEDs arranged in a 2-row × 5-column grid (or equivalent). Each LED is a small circle (~12px) that illuminates when its industry is selected. The label sits to the right of each LED in mono micro-type (10–11px, letter-spaced).

## 7. Data Spine Strip (Bottom)

The horizontal strip across the bottom of the center area visualizes the **Pimcore Data Spine** as a live signal-flow diagram. This is one of the cockpit's most important elements: it makes the architectural story visible without requiring a slide.

### 7.1 Structure

The spine strip occupies a region roughly 180px tall × full center-area width. It is laid out as three horizontal lanes:

```
INPUTS ─────────────────────→  THE SPINE  ─────────────────────→ OUTPUTS

  [ERP]                                                           [WEB]
  [PLM]    ╲                                                   ╱  [APP]
  [SUPPLIERS]──┐                                          ┌──   [MARKETPLACE]
  [WEB FORMS]──┤   ┌─────────────────────────────┐       ├──   [PRINT]
  [IoT]        ├──→│ ◆ PIM  ◆ MDM  ◆ DAM  ◆ CDP  │──→────┤      [PARTNERS]
  [LEGACY]   ──┘   │      ◆ DXP   ◆ COMMERCE     │       └──   [AGENTS]
                   └─────────────────────────────┘
                       GOVERNED · AGENTIC · SOVEREIGN
```

- **Left edge — Inputs.** Six labeled source nodes (ERP, PLM, Suppliers, Web Forms, IoT, Legacy) feeding flow lines toward the center.
- **Center — The Spine.** A single elongated horizontal channel containing six small **capability waypoints** (◆ glyphs) — one per Pimcore capability module. Below the channel, the three governance markers: `GOVERNED · AGENTIC · SOVEREIGN`. The spine itself is rendered as a soft-gradient horizontal pill shape.
- **Right edge — Outputs.** Six labeled output channels (Web, App, Marketplace, Print, Partners, Agents) receiving flow lines from the center.

### 7.2 Static behavior (no capabilities active)

- Inputs and outputs are visible but in muted gray.
- The spine pill itself is faintly visible — pale lavender, quiet.
- All capability waypoints are dim (◇ rather than ◆).
- No flow lines are illuminated.
- Governance markers are visible but at low opacity.

### 7.3 Capability-driven behavior

When a capability module is on:
- That capability's waypoint inside the spine illuminates in its accent color (◆).
- A subset of input → spine flow lines illuminates, weighted toward the inputs that capability consumes (e.g., PIM consumes ERP/PLM/Suppliers strongly; DXP consumes Web Forms; CDP consumes Web Forms/IoT).
- A subset of spine → output flow lines illuminates similarly (e.g., Commerce drives Marketplace and Web; DXP drives Web/App; PIM drives all output channels).

The mapping of capability → input bias and capability → output bias is editorial and lives in a small JSON config. See Section 9.4.

### 7.4 Agents-driven behavior

When the Agents knob is engaged (>0%):
- Small amber **orchestration pulses** begin traveling along all illuminated flow lines, from inputs through the spine to outputs.
- Pulse frequency scales with knob value: ~1 pulse/4s at 25%, ~1 pulse/s at 100%.
- At ≥80%, the spine pill itself takes on a soft amber halo, and the `AGENTIC` governance marker illuminates in amber.
- At 100%, a small `AGENTIC ORCHESTRATION` callsign appears above the spine.

### 7.5 PXM-engaged behavior

When all six modules are on (PXM Mode Engaged):
- All six capability waypoints inside the spine are illuminated.
- The spine pill takes on a unified Pimcore Purple gradient.
- All flow lines are illuminated.
- The `GOVERNED · AGENTIC · SOVEREIGN` markers all illuminate.
- Combined with high Agents value, this is the "Agentic PXM" final state — the strategic endpoint of the platform thesis.

### 7.6 Interactive behavior

The spine strip is **non-interactive** in v1 — it's a live readout, not a control. Hovering over an input, waypoint, or output could optionally surface a tooltip in v2 (e.g., hover ERP → "Product master, supplier feeds, transactional context").

```
state = {
  selectedIndustry:     'manufacturing',
  activeCapabilities:   Set<DomainSlug>,    // 0–6
```

Maintained as JSON files alongside the cockpit:

- **Industry benefits** (per industry, 3–5 strings) — see v2 §10.1.
- **Persona logic** (per capability mix archetype) — see §8.2 above.
- **Spine input/output bias** (per capability domain, scoring 0–1 against each spine input and each spine output) — drives which flow lines illuminate when a capability is on. Editorial. Example excerpt:

  ```json
  {
    "pim": {
      "inputs":  { "erp": 1.0, "plm": 0.9, "suppliers": 0.8, "web_forms": 0.1, "iot": 0.0, "legacy": 0.7 },
      "outputs": { "web": 1.0, "app": 0.9, "marketplace": 1.0, "print": 1.0, "partners": 0.8, "agents": 0.6 }
    },
    "cdp": {
  ```

### 10.2 Knob construction (skeuomorphic detail)

A single knob is built from layered SVG circles:

1. **Outer shadow** — soft drop shadow on the chassis (15–25px blur, low opacity).
2. **Outer ring** — slightly inset gradient simulating a metallic bevel.
3. **Knob body** — radial gradient from `--pc-metal-light` (top-left) to `--pc-metal-edge` (bottom-right), with a soft brushed-metal texture overlay (radial line pattern at 8–10% opacity).
4. **Highlight crescent** — thin off-white arc on the upper-left edge simulating a light source from upper-left.
5. **Pointer notch** — a small dot (~5% of knob radius) positioned at the current rotation angle, in `--pc-metal-dark`.
6. **Tick marks** — small marks around the perimeter, in `--pc-text-faint`.
7. **Halo ring** — colored arc(s) outside the knob body, in the relevant module accent or platform color.

This construction can be encapsulated in a single React component `<Knob>` parameterized by size, value, and accent color, and reused for all knobs in the cockpit (hero knobs and module sub-knobs).

### 10.3 Typography

| Role | Font | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Display, module names, hero labels | Inter or SF Pro Display | Geometric, premium, neutral. Falls back to system stack. |
| Mono readouts, tick labels, ICP fields | JetBrains Mono | Crisp, technical, instrument-cluster feel. |

Module names: 600 weight, letter-spaced 0.05em. Mono labels: 500 weight, letter-spaced 0.12em. Body text: 400 weight.

### 10.4 Motion principles

- **Restraint over abundance.** One coordinated reveal on load (staggered fade-in of regions over ~600ms).
- **Knob rotation.** Smooth, with a subtle inertial feel. 200–300ms eased transition when set programmatically.
- **Halo fills.** When a module turns on, its halo arc on the PXM knob fills in 350ms with a slight ease-out.
- **Connection lines.** Illuminate via opacity transition (300ms) plus a one-shot brightness pulse on first activation (150% intensity at peak, dropping to steady state in 600ms).
- **Spine pulses.** Continuous loop, 4–8s per pulse cycle depending on Agents knob value.
- **PXM Mode Engaged.** When the sixth module activates, the halo ring transitions from segmented colors to unified Pimcore Purple over 800ms, and a faint chassis-wide glow appears for 400ms before settling.

### 10.5 Texture & atmosphere

- **Chassis grain.** Very subtle film-grain noise overlay at 2–3% opacity over the chassis, gives it a tactile feel.
- **Soft shadows everywhere.** Every card, every knob, every meter floats above the chassis with realistic soft shadows. Shadow direction is consistent (light source upper-left, ~30°).
- **No hard borders.** Borders are conveyed via shadow and color-tint differential rather than explicit lines.
- **Connection lines** are the only explicit line elements. They contrast intentionally against the otherwise-soft surface design.

## 11. Interaction Design

### 11.1 Default state on load

```
selectedIndustry   = 'manufacturing'
activeCapabilities = { 'pim' }              // first module on
capabilityOrder    = [pim, mdm, dam, cdp, dxp, ecommerce]
agentsIntensity    = 15                     // knob slightly turned, "ASSIST" mode
activeLocale       = browser preference or 'en'
```

This produces a non-empty default scene: Manufacturing selected, PIM module active, the PXM knob halo showing one blue arc, the connection line from PXM to PIM module illuminated, the Agents knob slightly turned showing "ASSIST" mode, and a faint orchestration pulse already visible on the spine. The viewer immediately sees the cockpit "in use."

### 11.2 Interactions

- **Click an industry LED** — replaces selected industry. Updates: ICP, benefits, case study filter and reordering.
- **Click a module power button** — toggles that module. Updates: PXM knob halo arc, connection line, capability headlines panel, persona panel, spine waypoint and flow lines, case study highlights.
- **Drag the PXM knob** — engages capabilities progressively per `capabilityOrder`, powering on / off the corresponding module cards in sync.
- **Drag the Agents knob** — sets agents intensity 0–100. Updates: spine pulse frequency, agent connection-line pulses, ICP wedge readout (at full + agents > 50% → "AGENTIC PXM"), persona table.
- **Click a case study card** — opens canonical Pimcore URL in new tab.
- **Click the locale gear** — opens locale switcher.
- **Hover a knob** — subtle scale increase (1.02x) and shadow lift.

### 11.3 Knob interaction model

Knobs are dragged in a circular gesture (mouse-down on knob, drag in arc to rotate) OR in a vertical gesture (drag up = increase, drag down = decrease — common in audio plugins, more accessible for touch). The cockpit supports both. Shift-drag for fine adjustment. Double-click resets the knob to default.

### 11.4 Keyboard shortcuts (v1.5)

- `1`–`6` toggle modules.
- `←` / `→` arrow keys cycle industries.
- `↑` / `↓` arrow keys adjust Agents in 5% increments.
- `Space` toggles all six capabilities (engage / disengage PXM mode).
- `R` resets to default state.

## 12. Implementation Notes

### 12.1 Stack

A single-file HTML application with React 18 + Tailwind via CDN, JSX compiled in-browser with Babel Standalone. Same rationale as v2:
- Zero build step.
- Full design freedom via CDN Tailwind (arbitrary values needed for the skeuomorphic gradients).
- Single-file portability.

The two JSON exports load alongside via `fetch` or are inlined at refresh time.

### 12.2 Skeuomorphic rendering

The brushed-aluminum knob and soft-shadow card aesthetics are achievable purely in CSS + SVG. Specifically:

- **Knob faces** — SVG `<circle>` with radial gradient fills and `feGaussianBlur` filter for the brushed texture.
- **Soft shadows** — CSS `box-shadow` with multi-layer values (a tight inner shadow for definition, a wide soft shadow for depth).
- **Connection lines** — SVG `<path>` with stroke gradients and CSS `filter: drop-shadow()` for glow.
- **Spine flow lines** — animated SVG paths with `stroke-dashoffset` animation for the orchestration pulses.

No external graphics library required. Recommended: a lightweight wrapper component for `<Knob>` to encapsulate the SVG construction.

### 12.3 Scaling for 16:9

Render at fixed 1920×1080 internal resolution; apply `transform: scale(s)` where `s = min(width/1920, height/1080)`. Letterbox bars in chassis color (not pure black — pure black would clash with the warm off-white aesthetic).

### 12.4 Data flattening

Same as v2 §14.3 — pre-flatten the case study JSON to drop unused fields (logos, gallery images, full WYSIWYG bodies), shrinking from ~15MB to ~1–2MB.

### 12.5 Future-state hooks (not v1)

- **Cortex panel** — once Cortex naming is committed, a small dedicated readout near the Agents knob showing Cortex learning state. The Spine could carry an additional governance marker (`GOVERNED · AGENTIC · SOVEREIGN · LEARNING`).
- **Live counters** — header stats currently static; could pull from a live API.
- **A/B comparison** — the header A/B button is cosmetic in v1; in v2 it could store a configuration and let the viewer compare two states side-by-side.
- **Capability deep-dive modal** — the "EXPAND" link in the headlines panel opens a full per-capability detail view.
- **URL state** — encode state in URL for shareable deep-links (e.g., `?ind=manufacturing&caps=pim,dam,dxp&agents=80`).
- **Sound design** — subtle clicks on knob detents, soft hum at high Agents intensity. Most demo settings have no audio output; design for muted-by-default.

## 14. Open Questions

1. **Cortex inclusion timing.** Hold for v2 until naming is committed, or include now as an unnamed "learning layer" indicator? Recommended: hold.
2. **Header stat values.** Investor numbers (€13M ARR, 3,000+ installs) versus general numbers (542 customer stories, 17 industries, 6 domains)? Recommended: ship a config flag, default to general, switch for investor sessions.
3. **Knob interaction model.** Circular drag, vertical drag, both? Recommended: both, since vertical drag is more accessible and circular drag is more "in the metaphor."
4. **PXM knob engagement order configurability.** Drag-to-reorder modules in the rail (which changes the PXM knob's engagement order) — v1 or v2? Recommended: v2. Default order in v1 is sufficient.
5. **Module sub-knobs functional vs cosmetic.** Cosmetic in v1 keeps the spec tight; functional gives the cockpit more depth. Recommended: cosmetic v1, light functional v2 (e.g., the PIM "Channels" knob cycling through scenarios that adjust the right-panel content).
6. **Spine input/output labels.** Six inputs (ERP, PLM, Suppliers, Web Forms, IoT, Legacy) and six outputs (Web, App, Marketplace, Print, Partners, Agents) feel right for v1 — but should they vary by industry? E.g., Healthcare might show "EHR, HL7, Clinical Systems" inputs. Recommended: industry-invariant for v1; industry-specific for v2.
7. **Agents knob default position.** 0% (off, demonstrates the "before agents" baseline)? 15% (slight engagement, immediately shows the spine's orchestration pulses on first load)? Recommended: 15% — the cockpit should be alive on load, and the Agents story is core to Pimcore's forward thesis.

## 15. Success Criteria

The cockpit succeeds if, in a live investor or prospect conversation, the viewer:

1. Understands within 30 seconds that Pimcore is *one platform with six capabilities*, not six products — the connection lines from the PXM knob to the modules carry this message visually before any words are spoken.
2. Understands within 60 seconds that **agents orchestrate across the entire platform** — not bolted on as a feature — proven by the Agents knob being a first-class control and the spine's orchestration pulses.
3. Sees the combinatorial story land — capabilities used together produce more than their sum.
4. Recognizes their own industry's use case story without it being narrated to them.
5. Remembers two distinct visual moments: **PXM Mode Engaged** (six capabilities active, halo unified in Pimcore Purple, full connection-line fan) and **Agentic PXM** (high agents intensity, spine pulsing with orchestration).
6. Walks away with the impression that Pimcore as a *piece of engineering* feels premium — the craft signaling does work for the brand.

If the cockpit cannot reliably produce the first three within the first 60 seconds of unguided interaction, it has failed regardless of how beautiful it looks.
